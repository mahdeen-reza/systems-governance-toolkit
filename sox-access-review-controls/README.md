## Background

SOX ITGC access reviews require organizations to periodically verify that system access is appropriate, that reviews are completed by the designated reviewers, and that a clear audit trail exists demonstrating controls were executed. These reviews are tested externally — gaps in the control framework have direct audit consequences.

The reviews at the center of this project run in Google Sheets. Designated reviewers are assigned to rows corresponding to users under their management. Each reviewer must confirm or revoke access for their assigned users, and every decision must be attributable to the right person.

---

## The Problem

The access review process had a functioning framework, but the approach was detection-heavy — control gaps existed in how enforcement was applied and how attribution was tracked.

Reviewer assignment was documented, but nothing in the sheet enforced it at the point of entry. Approver validation happened after the fact, through a manual process of matching completed rows against HR data using spreadsheet formulas. Attribution relied on general edit history rather than cell-level tracking tied to the assigned reviewer for each row.

The gaps: **enforcement was not live, and attribution was not granular enough to satisfy the audit standard without manual reconstruction.**

---

## The Solution — Two Layers

The upgrade shifted the framework from detection-heavy to a multi-layered, control-heavy model with comprehensive action tracking. Two independent layers address the two gaps directly.

### Layer 1 — Row-Level Enforcement

Every row has a designated reviewer identified by their company Google Workspace email. Layer 1 enforces this assignment live at the point of entry:

- Protected columns can only be edited by the reviewer assigned to that specific row
- The editor's identity is captured automatically from their Google account login
- Unauthorized edits are reverted immediately and the editor is notified in real time
- Authorized edits are timestamped with the reviewer's email and time of sign-off

Approver validation is now continuous and automatic — the manual post-hoc step is eliminated.

### Layer 2 — Cell-Level Audit Trail

Layer 2 operates independently of Layer 1. On every edit attempt to the primary review decision column:

- A full record is appended to a dedicated audit log tab — authorized or not
- Fields captured: timestamp, editor email, record ID, user name, assigned reviewer email, value entered, match status, edit type
- A validation status is written to a visible column in the main sheet: `Match`, `No Match`, or `No Owner Assigned`
- Unauthorized attempts are captured **before** the Layer 1 revert fires — the audit record is complete even when enforcement succeeds

The two layers together satisfy both the enforcement and attribution requirements of a SOX ITGC access review. See [`docs/control_design.md`](docs/control_design.md) for full architectural rationale and design decisions.

## Impact

- **100% pass rate on SOX ITGC controls** with no discrepancies found since implementation
- Eliminated manual post-hoc approver validation — attribution is now live, continuous, and automatically recorded
- Audit evidence is generated by the process itself rather than reconstructed after the fact
- Deployed across User Access Reviews (UARs) and Privileged Access Reviews (PARs)
